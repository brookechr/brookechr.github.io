TEDx Talk

I worked on this project over the span of 6 weeks during my remote internship at Computer Enterprises, Inc. (CEI) during the summer of 2020. The aim of the project was to create a website that would allow employees at CEI to search a registry of other employees by their skills, certifications, industries of expertise, location, and employment type. Employees would be able to both create their own profile and browse others’ profiles.

For the first half, I worked alongside an intern with a digital design background. We used Figma to create low-fidelity, mid-fidelity, and high-fidelity prototypes. Our boss was our stakeholder for the project, so we worked under him and a senior UX designer, though for most of the time, it was just me and the other intern. The project evolved a lot between having to scrap a bunch of screens when we realized they wouldn’t fit in the MVP and switching the component library midway through.

Once we had completed the designs for the project, it was time to start developing. While the digital design intern moved onto another project, I was joined by another software engineering intern to work on the code. I did a lot of the front-end coding, using Angular with Angular Material and SCSS. Though the project wasn’t completed by the time I left, I learned so much. It was my first time really using version control software or working on any software project for so long, and I had never even heard of Angular or TypeScript when I started.

The following is a quote from the senior UX designer I worked with: “Her attention to detail was remarkable and her efficiency in doing both design and development were on par with some of the very best I have had the pleasure of working with over my career. Needless to say, I fully recommend and endorse Brooke to absolutely anyone in the software development industry. She is going to have an incredible career, one that I believe will be transformative to any company that is lucky enough to hire her.”
